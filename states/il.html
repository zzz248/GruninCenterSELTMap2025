üìã Illinois's Leadership Role

L3C Pioneer: Illinois was among the first 6 states to enact L3C legislation in 2009, helping to establish this innovative corporate form that bridges for-profit and nonprofit sectors.

Foundation Support: Illinois's L3C legislation was specifically designed to support program-related investments (PRIs) by foundations, creating new opportunities for philanthropic capital to support social enterprises.

Dual-Form Excellence: By adding benefit corporation legislation in 2014, Illinois created a comprehensive social enterprise ecosystem offering entrepreneurs multiple pathways to incorporate social mission into business structure.

Economic Impact: Illinois's early adoption of social enterprise legislation has positioned the state as an attractive jurisdiction for mission-driven businesses, impact investors, and social entrepreneurs.

National Influence: Illinois's experience with L3C legislation provided valuable insights that influenced the development of social enterprise law across the United States.

Business Environment: The availability of both L3C and BCORP forms gives Illinois businesses maximum flexibility in choosing corporate structures that align with their social missions and business objectives.
