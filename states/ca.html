SPC - Social Purpose Corporation
California Social Purpose Corporations Act
Cal. Corp. Code ¬ß¬ß 2500-3503 (2014)

Legislative Details

Originally enacted in 2011 as the Corporate Flexibility Act, establishing "flexible purpose corporations." In 2014, the act was renamed to the Social Purpose Corporations Act and the entities were renamed to "social purpose corporations." The statute was further amended in 2022 by SB 1202 to change voting requirements for mergers and exclude officers and directors from liability for forward-looking statements.

BCORP - Benefit Corporation
California Benefit Corporation
Cal. Corp. Code ¬ß¬ß 14600-14631

Legislative Details

Enacted in 2013 to provide an additional corporate form for businesses seeking to pursue both profit and general public benefit. California's benefit corporation statute follows the Model Benefit Corporation Legislation developed by B Lab.

üìã Additional Information

Legislative Effectiveness

California was a pioneer in social enterprise legislation, launching the Corporate Flexibility Act in 2011 (one of the earliest such laws) and adding the Benefit Corporation option in 2013. The state has shown continued commitment to refining and improving these corporate forms through targeted amendments.

Business Impact

California's two corporate form options serve different business models:

  • Social Purpose Corporations (SPC): More flexible approach allowing companies to define their own social/environmental purposes
  • Benefit Corporations (BCORP): Standardized approach requiring general public benefit and optional specific benefits

Regulatory Environment

California's regulatory framework provides clear guidance for both corporate forms. The state's mature legal infrastructure and experience with social enterprises makes it an attractive jurisdiction for mission-driven businesses and impact investors.

National Influence

As one of the first states to authorize social enterprise corporate forms, California's legislation has influenced similar laws in other states. The state's approach to balancing flexibility (SPC) with standardization (BCORP) has been studied and adapted by other jurisdictions.
